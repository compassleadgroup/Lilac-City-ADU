## 1. WHAT THIS SITE IS
A marketing and matching service that connects Spokane-area homeowners with licensed local ADU builders. It is not a builder, designer, or permit service. It earns by referring qualified homeowner leads to a builder tenant. It is free to the homeowner. It ranks through classic organic search only, with no Google Business Profile and no reliance on the map pack.

An ADU (accessory dwelling unit) is a smaller, self-contained second home on the same lot as a primary house: a detached backyard unit, an attached addition, a garage conversion, a basement unit, or a junior ADU. Spokane-area homeowners build them for rental income, multigenerational living, guest or office space, and added property value.

## 6. REGULATORY CONTEXT (RESEARCH LEADS, NOT FACTS, VERIFY EVERYTHING)
All figures below are unverified leads from third-party summaries or a stale 2016 city PDF. Confirm each against the primary source before publishing. Flag every conflict with HB 1337.

STATE LAW, HB 1337 (2023):
- Reported to require Growth Management Act jurisdictions inside Urban Growth Areas to allow up to two ADUs per lot, to limit owner-occupancy requirements, and to limit parking requirements near major transit, with minimum size allowances.
- Spokane is described as a Growth Management Act city in an Urban Growth Area, so HB 1337 applies fully. Reported compliance deadline was June 30, 2025.
- Primary sources to confirm: Washington HB 1337 text, RCW 36.70A, WA Department of Commerce ADU guidance (commerce.wa.gov).

CITY OF SPOKANE (confirm against spokanecity.org municipal code and Development Services ADU materials):
- A 2016 city overview PDF stated only one ADU per lot, a front setback figure, a five foot ADU side setback, one additional parking space, and that the ADU cannot exceed the primary dwelling. TREAT THE ONE-ADU LIMIT AND SETBACK AND PARKING FIGURES AS LIKELY STALE post-HB-1337. Verify all.
- A third-party summary (GatherADU, not a primary source) suggested detached ADU up to 800 sq ft or 75 percent of the primary, attached up to 800 or 50 percent, junior ADU up to 500, 20 foot height, one parking space, owner occupancy. Owner occupancy is very likely overridden by HB 1337. Verify every figure.
- Useful verified fact: the City of Spokane Development Services Center address is 808 West Spokane Falls Boulevard, Spokane, WA 99201-3336 (from the city permit document). Confirm before use.

UNINCORPORATED SPOKANE COUNTY (confirm against spokanecounty.gov):
- Building and Planning sheets BP-75 (detached ADU) and BP-76 (attached ADU) are the primary documents.
- County zoning is described as allowing a small second dwelling on the same parcel, with an off-street parking expectation for the ADU in addition to the main residence. Verify specifics.
- Note that cities inside the county (Spokane, Spokane Valley, Cheney, Liberty Lake, Airway Heights) have their own codes, so the homeowner's jurisdiction must be identified first.

CITY OF SPOKANE VALLEY (confirm against spokanevalleywa.gov):
- Has its own permit center and municipal code. Pull the current ADU standards directly.

SUPPORTING (not primary for rules, may be cited as homeowner resources): The ZONE Spokane ADU fact sheet.
